## Effect Overview

The left-side recording indicator should become a single circular dynamic orb that feels like a suspended glass energy core. It should not read as a meter, equalizer, or five-dot widget. The orb must feel alive even in silence, then visibly “heat up” when the user speaks.

The intended impression is:
- calm and premium at rest
- responsive and fluid during speech
- hotter, brighter, and more energized as volume increases

## Visual Identity

### Core shape
- A visually circular orb, not a capsule fragment and not a radial equalizer
- The outer silhouette should remain stable and close to a perfect circle
- The orb should look like a polished translucent object suspended inside the left slot of the floating capsule

### Material feel
- Glass-like or crystal-like, with depth rather than a flat fill
- A soft highlight on the upper edge or upper-left region
- Slight internal haze or fluid luminosity to imply volume inside the orb
- A restrained outer glow that expands with energy but does not become blurry noise

## Motion Language

### Idle state
- The orb should never look dead or fully static
- Internal energy should drift slowly, as if liquid light is circulating inside
- The dominant color should stay in a cool blue or blue-cyan family
- Scale pulsing, if any, should be extremely subtle

### Speaking state
- As RMS rises, internal motion should accelerate and become more noticeable
- The inner glow should brighten and gain contrast
- The orb may pulse slightly larger, but the outer contour should still read as circular and controlled
- Color should begin warming from blue into violet or magenta before reaching red

### Peak-energy state
- Loud speech should push the orb into its hottest expression
- Red or orange-red accents should appear clearly, but still feel luminous rather than flat
- The glow should intensify and the internal flow should feel more turbulent
- Even at peak energy, the orb should remain elegant rather than explosive

## RMS Mapping

Use RMS as a continuous energy value, not as a discrete state switch.

Suggested visual ramp:

| RMS Range | Dominant Feel | Color Direction | Motion |
|-----------|----------------|-----------------|--------|
| 0.00-0.15 | Idle listening | Blue / cyan | Slow inner drift |
| 0.15-0.40 | Light speech | Blue-violet | Noticeable fluid motion |
| 0.40-0.70 | Active speech | Violet-magenta with warm highlights | Stronger pulse and brighter glow |
| 0.70-1.00 | Peak energy | Orange-red / red accents over luminous core | Fastest internal motion and strongest glow |

This ramp is directional guidance, not a rigid palette lock. The key rule is that louder input must read as hotter energy.

## Composition Inside the Floating Capsule

- The orb should occupy a left slot that is visually wide and tall enough to read as near-circular
- It should stay vertically centered relative to the transcript block
- It should feel integrated with the capsule, not pasted on top of it
- The capsule itself should remain secondary to the orb when recording begins, but the orb should not overpower transcript readability

## Anti-Goals

Avoid the following:
- A direct Siri clone
- A blob that deforms so much it stops reading as a circle
- A flat solid red disk at high volume
- Equalizer bars, radial ticks, or segmented meter metaphors
- Aggressive strobing, flicker, or alarm-like color jumps

## Review Criteria

The effect is successful when:
- users can tell the app is listening even before speech starts
- louder speech feels visibly hotter and more energized
- the orb still looks refined at small size
- the left side of the floating capsule feels like a designed object, not a generic waveform widget
